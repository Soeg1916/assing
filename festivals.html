Festivals in Dessie

Festivals & Celebrations

Cultural Celebrations

Throughout the year, Dessie comes alive with vibrant festivals that showcase Ethiopia's rich cultural and religious heritage. These celebrations offer visitors a glimpse into the traditions, music, dance, and cuisine that have been preserved for generations.

Timkat

The Ethiopian Orthodox celebration of Epiphany, typically held in January. The city's streets fill with processions as priests carry replicas of the Ark of the Covenant, followed by dancing, singing, and ceremonial baptisms.

January

Eid al-Fitr

Marking the end of Ramadan, this important Islamic festival is celebrated by Dessie's significant Muslim population. Communal prayers, charitable giving, and festive meals with family and friends are central to the celebrations.

Varies (Islamic calendar)

Meskel

This festival commemorates the finding of the True Cross. In Dessie, as throughout Ethiopia, large bonfires (demera) are lit, accompanied by singing, dancing and feasting. The holiday falls at the end of the rainy season.

September 27

Ethiopian New Year (Enkutatash)

Celebrated in September, marking the end of the rainy season. In Dessie, the day is marked with new clothes, fresh flowers, singing, and traditional meals shared with family and neighbors.

September 11

Eid al-Adha

An important Islamic holiday honoring Abraham's willingness to sacrifice his son. The festival involves prayer, charitable giving, and the sharing of meat with family, friends, and those in need.

Varies (Islamic calendar)

Genna (Ethiopian Christmas)

Celebrated on January 7th according to the Ethiopian Orthodox calendar. In Dessie, the day begins with early morning church services followed by family gatherings and festive meals featuring traditional Ethiopian dishes.

January 7

Visitor Tips

If you're planning to visit Dessie during one of these festivals:

  • Book accommodation well in advance as hotels fill quickly during major festivals
  • Dress modestly, especially when attending religious ceremonies
  • Ask permission before photographing celebrations or participants
  • Consider hiring a local guide who can explain the cultural significance of the festivities
  • Be prepared for businesses to operate on reduced hours during major holidays
