People & Lifestyle

Daily Life in Dessie

Dessie offers visitors a glimpse into authentic Ethiopian highland life. The rhythm of daily activities, from morning coffee ceremonies to evening social gatherings, reflects the community-oriented spirit that characterizes Ethiopian culture.

The city comes alive early, with markets bustling from dawn and people beginning their workdays. The temperate highland climate allows for a comfortable pace of life, with outdoor activities and social interactions forming the backbone of community life.

Hospitality & Community

Visitors to Dessie often remark on the extraordinary hospitality of its residents. Being invited into a local home for coffee or a meal is not uncommon, reflecting the Ethiopian value of "ferenj yemeskel" (treating guests with honor).

Community ties are strong, with neighbors participating in traditional mutual aid systems like "iddir" (funeral associations) and "equb" (rotating savings groups) that have sustained Ethiopian social fabric for generations.

Traditional Clothing

Many Dessie residents blend modern attire with traditional Ethiopian clothing. On special occasions, you'll see women wearing beautiful "habesha kemis" (traditional embroidered dresses) and men in "netela" (white cotton shawls with colorful borders).

Greetings & Customs

Traditional greetings involve multiple handshakes and shoulder bumps between friends. Respect for elders is paramount, and it's customary to greet older people first. Showing respect through slight bows when greeting elders is still practiced.

Coffee Culture

Ethiopia is the birthplace of coffee, and in Dessie, the coffee ceremony remains a cherished daily ritual. The multi-hour process of roasting, grinding, and brewing coffee creates spaces for conversation and community bonding.

Religious Practices

Religious devotion shapes daily life for many residents. The calls to prayer from mosques and the bells from Orthodox churches mark the rhythm of the day, while Wednesday and Friday fasting is observed by many Orthodox Christians.
